About the African Medical Registry

Securing Healthcare Integrity Across Africa

What Is the African Medical Registry?

The African Medical Registry (AMR) is the continent's first unified digital platform that allows governments, hospitals, patients, embassies, and partners to verify the credentials and license status of healthcare practitioners across Africa.

AMR bridges national regulatory systems into a centralized yet country-integrated verification infrastructure, offering transparency, data security, and public confidence in medical professionals.

Our Vision

To build Africa's trusted digital identity system for healthcare — where every practitioner is digitally verifiable, ethically accountable, and professionally empowered.

Our Mission

To establish a secure, continent-wide health registry that digitally verifies all licensed healthcare practitioners and enables cross-border professional trust.

Strategic Objectives

Combat Credential Fraud

Eliminate fake practitioners across Africa's health sector

Real-time Verification

Instant QR code verification for all practitioners

Regulatory Integration

Unite medical, nursing, and pharmacy councils

Continental Scale

Build infrastructure for all 54 African nations

Our Story

The Beginning

AMR was founded in response to a continent-wide crisis of unlicensed and fake healthcare providers posing as professionals.

Ghana Pilot

Drawing lessons from Ghana's successful medical registry pilot, AMR emerged to unite Africa's fragmented health regulation systems.

Continental Growth

Today, AMR works with medical councils and governments across Africa, building the continent's most ambitious health governance infrastructure.

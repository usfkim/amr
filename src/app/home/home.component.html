What Is the African Medical Registry?

The African Medical Registry (AMR) is a continent-wide digital platform that enables citizens, hospitals, governments, and embassies to verify the identity and licensing status of healthcare professionals across Africa.

Patient Safety

Fake doctors and unlicensed practitioners are a silent threat. AMR empowers citizens to confirm their health provider is truly certified.

Easy Public Access

Through QR codes and online search, AMR makes professional verification quick and accessible across all 54 African nations.

Continental Integration

AMR unites regulators, governments, and countries on one secure, transparent digital registry.

Who Can Be Verified on AMR?

Medical Doctors & Dentists

Licensed physicians and dental practitioners across Africa.

Nurses & Midwives

Registered nursing professionals and certified midwives.

Medical Herbalists

Certified traditional medicine practitioners and herbalists.

Pharmacists

Licensed pharmaceutical professionals and specialists.

Laboratory Scientists

Certified laboratory and allied health professionals.

Mental Health

Licensed psychologists and mental health therapists.

Scan. Verify. Trust.

Every AMR-registered practitioner receives a QR code ID. Citizens can scan this code to instantly check license status, discipline history, and registration.
